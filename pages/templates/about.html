About Cardiovascular Illness Prediction

Our predictive model assesses the risk of cardiovascular illness based on several key factors:

  • Age in years: The age of the individual is a significant predictor of cardiovascular risk. As age increases, the risk of developing cardiovascular illness also tends to increase.
  • Height in centimeters: Height is considered as one of the parameters for assessing cardiovascular risk, as it can influence factors such as body mass index (BMI).
  • Weight in kilograms: Weight is another important factor in assessing cardiovascular risk. Higher body weight, especially when combined with other risk factors, can increase the likelihood of developing cardiovascular illness.
  • Systolic and Diastolic Blood Pressure (mmHg): Blood pressure readings are crucial indicators of cardiovascular health. Elevated blood pressure, both systolic and diastolic, is associated with an increased risk of cardiovascular illness.
  • Smoking status: Smoking is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease. Individuals who smoke are at a significantly higher risk of developing cardiovascular illness compared to non-smokers.
  • Physical activity level: Regular physical activity is associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular disease. Being active for at least one hour per day can contribute to overall cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of illness.

By considering these factors, our model provides predictions on the likelihood of an individual developing cardiovascular illness, helping users make informed decisions about their health.
